About Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds is a software engineer who initiated the development of the Linux kernel and Git. He was born on December 28, 1969, in Helsinki, Finland. ...

Linus Torvalds' influence extends beyond his own creations to the broader culture of software development. His dedication to open-source principles has inspired countless developers to contribute to open-source projects, fostering an environment where innovation and collaboration are paramount. This culture has led to the creation of numerous projects that form the backbone of the internet and modern computing.

Despite the massive success of Linux, Torvalds is known for his modesty and has often downplayed his personal contributions, highlighting the collaborative effort involved in the kernel's development. He has been quoted saying that he's not a visionary or a prophet; he just found a niche that needed to be filled.

The personal philosophy and ethos that Torvalds embodies have served as a guiding beacon for many in the software industry. His belief in the power of open, collaborative work has not only changed the way software is developed but has also had a democratizing effect on technology, making powerful software tools accessible to everyone.
